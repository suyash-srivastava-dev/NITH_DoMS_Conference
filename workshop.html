Workshop

Much of the quantitative research in management and social sciences deals with latent constructs - their operationalization, and their inter-relationships. The operationalization process predominantly involves either development of measure(s) for new construct(s) or refinement of existing measure(s) for given construct(s). This workshop intends to cover both of these aspects with special focus on developing new measures. The motivation behind conducting this workshop is based on the philosophy that in a research anything can be measured and can be managed effectively. In the development of a scale, we start from an abstract phenomenon (generally called concept), define its meaning and scope, and develop its measure. It is a multi-stage scientific process which incorporates qualitative ways to set up the conceptual scope of phenomenon and robust quantitative assessments to establish reliability and validity. Majority of scales being used in Indian context are mainly adapted versions of western scales, which do not fully capture the contextual reality of the phenomenon. There have been calls in the past for developing India-specific measures for various constructs (management and social science). While, few attempts have been made by Indian scholars in this direction but lot more needs to be done. This workshop will offers a comprehensive and in-depth understanding of scale construction and development process. Session overview with an understanding of scale development process and procedure. More specifically, we will explore how and when scale development is required in social science research. The workshop would be useful for the faculties, trainers, researchers, and professionals.

Objectives of e-Workshop


  • To provide hands-on training on scale development process with application of SPSS and AMOS for exploratory and confirmatory factor analysis, respectively.
  • To explain both philosophical and technical aspects of scale development; advanced scale validation techniques –nomological, known group validation, etc.
  • To expose the participants to the process of adapting existing scales in research

Topics to be covered

Need for Measurement scale, Types of Scales Formative vs. Reflective, Scale Development - Concept , Construct & Operationalization, Issues in Dimensionality –uni-dimension vs. multi-dimension constructs 1st order & 2nd order constructs, Identification of literature gap and Identifying dimensions –From literature, Qualitative data Secondary data, Developing items using literature, Expert validation - Content & Face Validity, Exploring factor analysis- Initial reliability& validity testing, Confirmatory Factor Analysis, Measurement Model Construction, CFA - Testing reliability and validity Fit indices, Convergent and Discriminant validity, Advanced scale validation: Nomological validity, Common Method Biasness, Plagiarism in Research, Ethics in Research and Publication, Selecting a Peer-Reviewed Journal for Quality Research, Publication of Quality Research in Reputed Journal
